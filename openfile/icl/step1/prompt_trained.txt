Revised key steps for the introduction section of an academic paper:

1. Clearly state the problem being addressed and its significance, including a concise statement of the main contribution of the paper.
2. Provide a clear motivation for the proposed method, highlighting the limitations of existing methods and how the proposed method addresses these limitations.
3. Give an overview of the paper, including a brief explanation of the proposed model's architecture and technical details.
4. Provide a comprehensive literature review of related works, highlighting the gaps in the existing literature, with a focus on Seq2Seq models, and including a comparison with other state-of-the-art methods in the field.
5. Present experimental results to demonstrate the effectiveness of the model and its contribution to the field.
6. Clearly state the performance of the proposed method and how it differs from existing methods.
