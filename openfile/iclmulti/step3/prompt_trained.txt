The introduction follows the structure of Revised key steps version 1. Here is the complete content of the guidance:

1. Provide context and background information, highlighting the research problem and objectives.
2. Conduct a comprehensive review of related work, emphasizing the limitations of existing models and explaining the main differences from your work.
3. Clearly state the research questions and objectives of the paper, and provide a clear thesis statement.
4. Clearly state the main contribution of the paper and the proposed solution, providing a clear motivation for the proposed approach.
5. Provide a clear overview of the methodology, experimental setup, and evaluation metrics used, emphasizing the strengths and limitations of the proposed approach and how they address the research questions.
6. Summarize the main contributions of the paper and its significance, emphasizing the novelty and importance of the proposed approach.
