The introduction follows the structure of Revised key steps version 1. Here is the complete content of the guidance:

1. Provide a clear motivation for the proposed model and explain its significance.
2. Clearly state the problem and the main contribution of the paper.
3. Give a comprehensive overview of related work, highlighting the limitations of previous models and the gaps in the existing literature that the proposed approach aims to address.
4. Explain the technical details of the proposed approach and provide a clear description of the experimental setup and evaluation metrics used in the paper, including a discussion of the results and their implications.
5. Clearly state the research questions or hypotheses that the paper aims to address and provide a clear justification for the proposed method.
6. Conclude by summarizing the main findings, highlighting the significance of the proposed method, and discussing future research directions.
