Sure, here is a guidance to write a good introduction section for an academic paper based on the given contributions:

1. Start with a general statement that introduces the topic of the paper and its significance. This statement should grab the reader's attention and provide context for the research.

2. Provide a brief overview of the research problem and the research question(s) that the paper aims to address. This should be done in a clear and concise manner.

3. Highlight the main contributions of the paper. This could include the research methods used, the theoretical framework, or the key findings. Be sure to explain why these contributions are important and how they advance the field.

4. Provide a brief outline of the paper's structure. This will help the reader understand how the paper is organized and what to expect in each section.

5. End the introduction with a clear and concise thesis statement that summarizes the main argument of the paper. This statement should be specific and focused, and should clearly state the paper's main contribution to the field.

By following these guidelines, you can write a strong and effective introduction that sets the stage for the rest of the paper.
